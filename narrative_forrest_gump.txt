Forrest Gump is a young boy growing up in Greenbow, Alabama. He wears leg braces and struggles to walk, but his mother always encourages him. He meets his best friend Jenny for the first time on the school bus.
---
Forrest's mother gives him wisdom every day. She often says, "Life is like a box of chocolates — you never know what you are going to get." Forrest listens carefully to every word she says.
---
Forrest runs for the first time without his braces. The braces break apart and he discovers he is incredibly fast. The boys chasing him are surprised and cannot catch him.
---
Forrest enters high school. A football coach watches him run across the field and is amazed at his speed. Forrest is recruited to play football because of his running ability.
---
Forrest becomes a star football player in college. He scores touchdowns with ease and the crowd cheers loudly. He meets the university president and shakes his hand after a big game.
---
Forrest graduates from college with a football scholarship. He has no clear plan for his future. A military recruiter approaches him at the graduation ceremony and offers him a path.
---
Forrest joins the United States Army. He meets his best friend Bubba on the bus to boot camp. Bubba talks endlessly about shrimp and all the ways you can cook it.
---
Forrest and Bubba complete basic training and are sent to Vietnam together. The jungle is hot and dangerous. Their drill sergeant, Sergeant Dan, is their commanding officer and he is very strict.
---
In Vietnam, Forrest's unit is ambushed in the jungle. There is gunfire all around them. Forrest acts without thinking and starts carrying wounded soldiers to safety one by one.
---
Forrest rescues many soldiers including Sergeant Dan, but Bubba is gravely wounded. Bubba dies in Forrest's arms in the jungle. Forrest is deeply saddened by the loss of his dear friend.
---
Forrest is awarded the Medal of Honor for his bravery in battle. He meets the President of the United States at the White House. He tells the President he really has to go to the bathroom.
---
While recovering from his wound, Forrest discovers a talent for ping-pong. He practices every day in the hospital. He becomes so skilled that he is invited to play in China as part of a cultural exchange.
---
Forrest returns to the United States as a ping-pong celebrity. He appears on television and in magazines. He is asked to endorse ping-pong paddles and becomes well known across the country.
---
Forrest remembers his promise to Bubba and decides to start a shrimping business. He buys an old boat and names it the Jenny, after his childhood friend. Sergeant Dan joins him as first mate.
---
The shrimping business is very difficult at first and they catch almost no shrimp. Forrest and Sergeant Dan work long days on the water with little success. They begin to lose hope after many empty nets.
---
A terrible hurricane destroys all the other shrimp boats in the area. Forrest's boat survives because it was far from shore. Suddenly they are the only shrimp boat left operating and demand is enormous.
---
The shrimping business becomes hugely successful. They catch enormous amounts of shrimp every day. Forrest and Sergeant Dan become rich beyond anything they had ever imagined.
---
Forrest's mother becomes very ill at home in Alabama. He returns to care for her in her final days. She tells him that death is just a part of life and not to be afraid.
---
After his mother passes away, Forrest is very sad and does not know what to do next. He decides to start running one day and just keeps going. He runs out of his neighborhood, through the town, and keeps going.
---
Forrest runs across Alabama, then across the whole state of Texas. People begin to follow him wondering why he is running. News reporters ask him if he is running for a cause or a charity.
---
Forrest keeps running for over three years without stopping. A group of followers joins him as he crosses the country. They think he must have a great message but he simply says he felt like running.
---
One day Forrest suddenly stops in the middle of nowhere. He tells his followers that he is tired and wants to go home. They are confused but Forrest just turns around and walks back.
---
Forrest receives a letter from Jenny, the girl he has loved since childhood. She invites him to come visit her. He travels to see her and is overjoyed to be reunited with his old friend.
---
Jenny tells Forrest that he has a son named Forrest Junior. Forrest is surprised and emotional. He sits next to his son and they watch television together quietly for the first time.
---
Forrest asks Jenny to marry him. She accepts his proposal and they have a small wedding at home. The ceremony is simple but full of love and attended by close friends and family.
---
Jenny's health worsens after the wedding. Forrest takes care of her every day with great devotion. He reads to her, cooks for her, and holds her hand through everything.
---
Jenny passes away in the spring, leaving Forrest alone to raise their son. Forrest stands by her grave under their old oak tree. He talks to her quietly as if she can still hear him.
---
Forrest raises his son with patience and love. He takes him to school on the bus every morning. He tells him the same wisdom his own mother taught him many years before.
---
On the first day of school, Forrest Junior boards the school bus with confidence. Forrest watches from the front of their house. He thinks about how far he has come from that same bus stop long ago.
---
Forrest sits on a bench and a white feather drifts past him in the breeze. He watches it float up into the blue sky. Life, he thinks, is a little bit luck and a little bit what you make of it.
