Satsuki and Mei move with their father to an old house in the Japanese countryside. Their mother is staying in a nearby hospital to recover from an illness. The house is surrounded by tall trees and green fields.
---
The girls explore the old house for the first time. They discover small black creatures called soot sprites hiding in the dark corners. The sprites scatter and disappear when sunlight reaches them.
---
The family works together to clean the dusty house. Their father explains that the soot sprites are harmless spirits who flee when people arrive. Satsuki and Mei feel excited about their new home.
---
Mei wanders into the garden alone while Satsuki is at school. She notices two small white rabbit-like creatures carrying a bag of seeds. She decides to follow them through the tall grass.
---
The small creatures lead Mei through a narrow tunnel beneath a giant camphor tree. She tumbles down into a hidden hollow and lands on the soft belly of a large sleeping creature. She is not afraid.
---
The creature opens one enormous eye and stares at Mei. She stares back with great curiosity and courage. She declares that he must be a Totoro and the creature yawns a wide and thunderous yawn.
---
Mei falls asleep curled beside Totoro in his warm nest under the roots. When she wakes up she finds herself back at the surface of the garden. She runs to tell her father what she discovered.
---
Satsuki returns from school and Mei tries to show her the tunnel in the garden. They search everywhere but cannot find it again. Satsuki is skeptical but does not dismiss her little sister's story.
---
One rainy night the girls wait alone at a dark bus stop for their father. He is late coming home from work. Satsuki holds an umbrella and Mei stands quietly beside her.
---
A large shape appears silently beside them at the bus stop. It is Totoro wearing a large leaf on his head as an umbrella. The girls are surprised but Satsuki bravely hands him a spare umbrella.
---
Totoro examines the umbrella with great wonder as if he has never seen one before. He blows a roar that shakes the treetops and summons the rain. He clearly enjoys the sound of raindrops on the umbrella.
---
A giant bus shaped like a cat appears on the empty road with twelve glowing eyes. The Catbus collects Totoro and disappears into the night. All that remains is the umbrella and a small bag of seeds.
---
The next morning the girls find the bag of seeds that Totoro left them. Their father encourages them to plant the seeds in the garden together. Satsuki and Mei dig small holes in the soft earth.
---
That night Totoro visits while the girls are sleeping. He dances and stamps on the ground above the seedlings with two small forest spirits. Strange music fills the air and the garden glows softly.
---
The girls wake and look outside to find that enormous trees have sprouted overnight in the garden. They climb to the top of the tallest tree in the moonlight with Totoro. The view stretches across the entire valley.
---
Satsuki goes to school every day and works hard at her lessons. She helps her younger sister feel less lonely by drawing pictures and telling stories. She tries to act brave even when she misses her mother.
---
Their father visits the mother in the hospital and the girls wait at home together. The house feels very quiet without him. Mei sits by the window and watches the road waiting for any sign of his return.
---
A message arrives saying their mother's condition has suddenly worsened. Satsuki and her father become very worried and speak in hushed voices. Mei overhears and does not fully understand what is happening.
---
Mei decides to walk to the hospital alone to bring fresh vegetables to her mother. She gets confused in the countryside and loses her way among rice paddies and narrow paths. She cannot find the road back.
---
Satsuki returns home and discovers that Mei is missing. She searches the fields calling her name. The neighbors help search but there is no sign of Mei anywhere in the village.
---
Satsuki runs desperately to the old camphor tree and calls out to Totoro for help. She begs him to help her find Mei. Totoro opens his wide mouth and lets out a tremendous roar into the sky.
---
The Catbus appears immediately and offers to help Satsuki. She climbs inside his warm furry body and they race across rooftops and treetops at tremendous speed. The countryside rushes past below them.
---
The Catbus carries Satsuki straight to where Mei is sitting alone on the road crying. Satsuki leaps out and embraces her little sister tightly. Mei is safe and unhurt and very relieved to see her big sister.
---
The Catbus changes its destination to the hospital where their mother rests. They travel through the air above the rice fields and rivers. The landscape passes quickly beneath the evening sky.
---
Satsuki and Mei peek through the hospital window and see their mother laughing with their father inside. She looks better and is sitting up in bed. The girls feel a flood of relief wash over them.
---
Their mother senses something and looks toward the window. For a moment she sees shadows in the tree nearby. She smiles and says she thought she just glimpsed Satsuki and Mei in the branches.
---
The Catbus takes the girls back home before anyone knows they were gone. They feel a deep gratitude and wave goodbye to the Catbus from outside the house. It vanishes instantly into the dark forest.
---
The next day a letter arrives saying their mother is well enough to come home soon. Their father reads the letter aloud at breakfast with great relief. The whole family feels hope return to the house.
---
Satsuki and Mei tend the garden where the giant tree once appeared. They prepare the house for their mother's homecoming with flowers and drawings. The countryside outside is warm and full of birdsong.
---
Totoro watches from high in the camphor tree as the family reunites in the yard below. He smiles quietly and disappears back into the forest. The spirit of the forest remains all around them as they live their lives.
