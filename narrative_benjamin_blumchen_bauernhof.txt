Benjamin Blümchen is a cheerful elephant who lives in the zoo in the city of Neustadt. He has a best friend named Otto who visits him every day after school. One sunny morning Otto arrives at the zoo with exciting news.
---
Otto tells Benjamin that his uncle Karl owns a big farm in the countryside. He has been invited to spend a whole week there and wants Benjamin to come along. Benjamin's trunk wiggles with happiness at the idea of an adventure.
---
The zookeeper Mr. Tierfreund gives Benjamin permission to visit the farm for the week. He packs a small bag with his favourite hay snacks and a yellow scarf. Otto and Benjamin set off together on the long road out of the city.
---
After a few hours of walking through green fields and forest paths they see the farm ahead. A large red barn stands behind a wooden gate with a painted sign that reads Hof Sonnenblume. Benjamin trumpets a happy greeting as they arrive.
---
Uncle Karl greets them warmly at the gate and shakes Otto's hand. He looks up at Benjamin with wide eyes and laughs with great surprise. He has never had an elephant as a guest on the farm before.
---
Uncle Karl shows them around the farmyard where chickens peck freely at the ground. A large dog named Bello runs up to sniff Benjamin's enormous feet. Benjamin stands very still and lets Bello sniff until the dog wags his tail in approval.
---
Benjamin and Otto are shown to a cozy barn where fresh straw has been laid out for sleeping. A row of small windows lets in golden afternoon light. Benjamin decides this is the most comfortable place he has ever slept outside the zoo.
---
Early the next morning a rooster crows loudly from the top of the fence post. Benjamin wakes up at once and lifts his trunk to feel the cool fresh air. He has never heard a rooster before and finds the sound absolutely wonderful.
---
Aunt Greta is in the kitchen making a large breakfast for everyone. She serves warm bread rolls, honey, and bowls of fresh milk from the farm. Benjamin gets an extra-large bowl of oats mixed with apple slices as a special welcome gift.
---
After breakfast Uncle Karl takes them to meet the cows in the meadow. There are six brown cows with large gentle eyes standing in the long grass. Benjamin touches one softly on the nose with his trunk and she moos in calm greeting.
---
Uncle Karl explains that the cows must be milked twice every day in the morning and in the evening. He shows Otto how to sit on the small stool and how to hold the bucket. Otto tries his best but the milk splashes everywhere and everyone laughs.
---
Benjamin watches the milking carefully and then asks politely if he may try. Uncle Karl looks puzzled but hands Benjamin the bucket. Benjamin uses his trunk so gently that a thin steady stream of milk flows perfectly into the pail and Uncle Karl cheers.
---
Next they visit the pig pen where five round pink pigs roll happily in the mud. The largest pig looks up at Benjamin with small curious eyes and grunts three times. Benjamin grunts back in a deep rumbling tone and the pig seems very satisfied.
---
Otto slips on the edge of the pig pen and tumbles face-first into the mud. He looks up with mud on his nose and glasses and laughs so hard his belly shakes. Benjamin uses his trunk as a hose and sprays clean water to wash him off.
---
Uncle Karl shows them the vegetable garden behind the farmhouse. Long rows of carrots, potatoes, beans, and tomatoes grow neatly in the dark earth. Benjamin helps to water the rows by filling his trunk at the well and spraying carefully along each line.
---
Aunt Greta asks Benjamin if he would like to help pick the ripe tomatoes. He uses his trunk to pluck each one with great delicacy and places them gently in the basket. Not a single tomato is squashed and Aunt Greta claps her hands in delight.
---
In the afternoon they walk out to the large wheat field that stretches to the edge of the forest. Uncle Karl explains that the wheat will be harvested in a few weeks for baking bread. Benjamin looks out over the golden field and feels a deep quiet happiness.
---
Otto finds a grasshopper in the wheat field and holds it up proudly on his finger. Benjamin lowers his head very slowly to look at the tiny green creature. The grasshopper leaps onto Benjamin's trunk and sits there for a long moment before jumping away.
---
Uncle Karl has a tractor and he lets Otto sit in the big seat and steer across the yard. Benjamin walks beside them and pretends to race the tractor by trotting very fast. The tractor wins but only just and everyone arrives at the barn breathless with laughter.
---
In the evening Aunt Greta makes a great pot of vegetable soup using things from the garden. They all sit around the long wooden table in the farmhouse kitchen. Benjamin's bowl is the size of a wash tub and he empties it completely and asks for more.
---
After dinner Uncle Karl brings out an old accordion and plays folk songs beside the fireplace. Otto claps along and Bello howls softly in a tone that makes them all laugh. Benjamin sways his large grey body from side to side as if dancing.
---
The next morning Benjamin wakes very early before the rooster crows. He walks quietly outside to watch the sunrise over the fields. The sky turns from dark blue to pink to bright gold and Benjamin decides it is the most beautiful thing he has ever seen.
---
Uncle Karl finds Benjamin standing in the meadow staring at the sunrise and brings him a bucket of warm oats. They stand together in silence watching the light grow stronger. Uncle Karl tells Benjamin that he loves the farm most in the early morning.
---
That day they help Uncle Karl repair a section of fence along the sheep pasture. Two fence posts have fallen over during a storm and the sheep keep wandering out. Benjamin pushes the heavy posts back into the ground with his forehead while Otto hammers the boards in place.
---
The sheep gather around Benjamin after the fence is fixed and one small lamb presses against his leg. Benjamin looks down at the lamb with great tenderness. He strokes it very gently with his trunk and it makes a soft bleating sound and closes its eyes.
---
Uncle Karl has a horse named Rosi who is large and light brown with a white star on her forehead. She is shy and usually stays at the far end of the paddock. Benjamin speaks to her very softly in a low rumbling voice and she walks slowly toward him.
---
Rosi allows Benjamin to stroke her long neck and she rests her head against his cheek. Uncle Karl watches from the fence with surprise because Rosi does not normally trust strangers. Benjamin and Rosi stand together for a long quiet moment in the morning sun.
---
Otto asks Uncle Karl if he may ride Rosi around the paddock. Rosi is gentle and steady and Otto holds the reins carefully. Benjamin walks alongside them making encouraging sounds and Rosi trots all the way around without stopping.
---
In the afternoon a delivery truck drives up to the farm with bags of animal feed. The driver is surprised to find an elephant helping to stack the heavy bags in the barn. Benjamin carries four bags at a time and the work is finished in half the usual time.
---
Aunt Greta makes apple cake in the afternoon and the smell drifts all the way out to the barn. Benjamin lifts his trunk and follows the scent directly to the kitchen window. Aunt Greta laughs and passes him a slice through the window on a large plate.
---
The next morning Uncle Karl needs to move the cows from the lower meadow to the upper field for fresh grass. Usually this takes two people because the cows wander in all directions. Benjamin walks calmly behind the herd and they follow his gentle trumpet calls in a perfect line.
---
One old cow named Mathilde refuses to move from her favourite spot under a pear tree. Uncle Karl tries calling her and Otto waves his arms but she does not budge. Benjamin whispers something very quietly into Mathilde's ear and she finally stands up and walks along.
---
Otto asks Benjamin what he said to Mathilde to make her move. Benjamin winks and says he told her there was fresher and sweeter grass waiting just over the hill. Otto shakes his head and tells Benjamin he can convince absolutely anyone of anything.
---
That afternoon a thunderstorm rolls in from over the forest. Dark clouds pile up quickly and the wind bends the tops of the tall trees. Uncle Karl asks Benjamin to help close all the barn doors and secure the hay bales before the rain arrives.
---
The storm breaks with a tremendous crack of thunder that rattles the farmhouse windows. All the animals on the farm grow restless and nervous in their stalls. Benjamin walks slowly from stall to stall making his low calming sound until every animal settles down.
---
The rain pours down for two hours and then stops as quickly as it came. The farmyard smells of wet earth and clean air after the storm. A rainbow appears over the wheat field and Benjamin points to it with his trunk.
---
The next day is market day in the nearby village and Aunt Greta packs baskets of eggs, jars of honey, and fresh vegetables onto the farm cart. Uncle Karl harnesses Rosi to pull the cart down the road. Benjamin and Otto walk alongside on the short journey to town.
---
The village market is full of farmers, children, and dogs running between the stalls. Everyone stops and stares when they see Benjamin walking calmly down the middle of the market square. A small girl points up at him and says he is even bigger than her house.
---
Benjamin helps Aunt Greta at her market stall by holding the sign high above the crowd so everyone can read it. Many customers come over out of curiosity and end up buying eggs and honey. Aunt Greta sells out completely for the first time in years.
---
A group of small children gather around Benjamin and ask him to lift them up one at a time with his trunk. Their parents watch nervously at first but Benjamin is so gentle and careful that they begin to smile. Each child gi ggles with joy as they rise slowly into the air.
---
The village baker gives Benjamin a giant pretzel as a thank-you for bringing so many customers to the market. Benjamin holds it up and examines it with his trunk before eating it in one enthusiastic bite. Everyone around him laughs and the baker immediately offers another.
---
On the way home from the market Otto finds a baby rabbit sitting alone at the edge of the road. It is very small and trembling and seems to have lost its mother. Otto carries it carefully in his cupped hands and they bring it back to the farm.
---
Aunt Greta makes a soft nest of hay and cotton in a small wooden box for the baby rabbit. Benjamin watches over it all evening very quietly and does not move from the corner of the kitchen. In the morning the rabbit is hopping around the box and eating small pieces of carrot.
---
Uncle Karl says the rabbit must be released back near the place where it was found once it is strong enough. Otto names it Schnuffi before the release and promises to remember it always. Benjamin gives Schnuffi one last gentle sniff and wishes it well in a soft rumble.
---
There is a large apple orchard on the far side of the farm that Uncle Karl has not had time to harvest. The apples are ripe and some are already falling from the branches. Benjamin offers to help and spends the whole morning reaching the highest branches with his trunk.
---
Otto and Aunt Greta gather the fallen apples into wooden crates below while Benjamin picks from above. By lunchtime the entire orchard has been harvested which would normally take three days. Uncle Karl says Benjamin has saved him a week of work and shakes his trunk warmly.
---
Aunt Greta makes fresh apple juice from the harvest and fills a row of glass bottles. She gives Benjamin a large jug all to himself and he drinks it in long slow sips. He declares it the finest juice he has ever tasted and Aunt Greta beams with pride.
---
That evening Uncle Karl says they must plant new seedlings in the freshly dug vegetable bed before the end of the week. He has bags of seeds for spinach, radishes, and onions waiting in the shed. Otto and Benjamin take turns making the rows while Uncle Karl drops in the seeds.
---
Benjamin notices that one section of the garden bed is too dry and the seeds will not grow well without water. He fills his trunk at the stream and gives the dry section a long careful watering. Uncle Karl marks that section with a stick as Benjamin's row.
---
On the last full day of their visit Uncle Karl takes them out to the forest at the edge of the farm. He knows a path through the trees that leads to a small pond where wild ducks nest in the reeds. Benjamin walks carefully so as not to frighten the birds.
---
At the pond a mother duck leads seven small ducklings across the still water in a perfect line. Benjamin freezes completely and watches with shining eyes. He stays motionless for so long that the ducks swim right toward him and one duckling climbs onto his foot.
---
Otto throws small pieces of bread into the water and the ducklings swim in quick circles chasing the crumbs. The mother duck watches Benjamin carefully and then goes back to her feeding. Benjamin understands this as a gesture of trust and feels very honoured.
---
On the way back through the forest Uncle Karl points out wild mushrooms, deer tracks, and a hollow tree where an owl sleeps in the daytime. Benjamin examines everything with his trunk and listens to all of Uncle Karl's explanations very attentively. Otto scribbles notes in his little notebook.
---
That evening the whole family sits outside on the long wooden bench in front of the farmhouse. The stars appear one by one in the clear country sky which is darker and fuller than the sky above the city. Benjamin counts the stars he knows and runs out of names.
---
Uncle Karl brings out glasses of warm milk and a plate of fresh cookies for the evening. He tells them stories about the farm when he was a young man and how his father taught him everything. Benjamin listens in complete silence and stores every word carefully in his long elephant memory.
---
Otto falls asleep against Benjamin's warm flank under the stars. Uncle Karl drapes a blanket over him and Aunt Greta brings a second one for good measure. Benjamin stays very still so as not to wake his friend and watches over him through the quiet night.
---
On the morning of their departure Aunt Greta packs a large basket of food for the journey home. There are rolls, carrots, apples, a jar of honey, and a small bottle of apple juice. Benjamin carries the basket on his trunk all the way along the farm path.
---
Uncle Karl and Aunt Greta stand at the gate to wave them off and Bello barks twice in farewell. Rosi the horse stands at the paddock fence and watches Benjamin go. Benjamin pauses and looks back at the farm one last time and lets out a long soft trumpet goodbye.
---
On the road back to the city Otto asks Benjamin what his favourite part of the week was. Benjamin thinks for a long time and then says it was all of it equally from the rooster to the stars. Otto nods and says he thinks the farm changed both of them a little.
---
Back in Neustadt the zookeeper Mr. Tierfreund comes out to welcome Benjamin home. He notices that Benjamin seems quieter and more thoughtful than before. Benjamin tells him that spending a week on the farm taught him how much he still has to learn about the world.
---
That night Benjamin stands in his enclosure and looks up at the stars above the zoo. He thinks about the cows, the sheep, the ducklings, and the smell of rain on warm earth. He closes his eyes and decides that whenever the city becomes too loud he will remember the farm and feel calm again.
